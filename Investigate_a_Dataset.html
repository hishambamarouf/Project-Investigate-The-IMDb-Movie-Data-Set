Introduction¶

In this Report, we are going to investigate the TMDb movie data set. The data set contains important information about 10,000 movies most notably: Popularity, Budget, Revenue, genre, vote count and director.

Based on these questions, we're going to ask the following questions and answer them at the end:
1- Which director makes the highest vote average.
2- Do high, or low budget movies get the highest vote average?
3- What genre is most popular?
4- Do longer movies get a higher vote average ?

Potential Issues:¶

1- Non-relavent columns: id, imdb_id, homepage, tagline (taglines usually affect how many people watch the movie and that is not provided in the datalist.) , overview, keywords(too specific.), release_date(since release_date will suffer from Y2K, i decided to go with release_year only).

Conclusions¶

We have explored this data set to answer the following questions:
1- Which director makes the highest vote average.
2- Do high, or low budget movies get the highest vote average?
3- What genre is most popular?
4- Do longer movies get a higher vote average ?

And based on my intuition, we find the following:
1- The best directors in the industry based on this dataset are Damien Chazelle, Pete Docter and Tony Kaye
2- There is no correlation between the budget of a movie and the vote average it gets.
3- The most popular genres of movies through time are Drama, comedy and thriller.
4- Longer movies corrolate to Highier vote average.

Limitations¶

Some of the limitations that hidnered my analysis are:
1-The sample size was too small since we had to drop about two thirds of the dataset duo to nulls, duplicates and bad values
2-Insufficient information about the movies, some of the things that we wanted to know are: Who is the star of the movie and how do they impact sales and votes.
3- there were too many outliers in the Popularity column which is why it wasn't used, the %25 is 0.207583 and min is 0.000065
